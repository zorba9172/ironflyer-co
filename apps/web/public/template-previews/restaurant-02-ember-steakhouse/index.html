The Fire

The hearth runs the kitchen.

Every protein on this menu touches the same fire. We burn white oak and pecan, split that morning, never gas, never propane. The hearth is lit at three in the afternoon and finally raked down at midnight.

Why one fire? Because the cook reads it. The cook adjusts. The cook decides when the ribeye comes off, not a timer.

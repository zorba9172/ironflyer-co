How it works

A LabLine week.

Three live sessions, one graded problem set, and weekly office hours with the instructor.

Step 01

Live lectures, recorded

Two 75-minute lectures a week, taught live on Zoom and Whiteboard. Recordings are posted within 24 hours.

Step 02

Graded problem sets

Every Friday you submit a problem set. Every Tuesday you get it back, marked up by a human grader with comments.

Step 03

Office hours, every week

One hour with your instructor and 60 minutes with a teaching assistant. Bring questions, leave with answers.

FAQ

Common questions.

What grade level is each course meant for?

Each course lists a target grade. Most students are between 10th grade and second-year university. We run a free 20-minute placement quiz so you can verify before you enroll.

Do you offer financial aid?

Yes. Roughly 30% of our students receive partial or full aid based on household income. The application takes about ten minutes and is reviewed in five business days.

Are courses synchronous or self-paced?

They are synchronous, but every lecture is recorded. About 20% of our students attend live lectures asynchronously. Problem-set deadlines are weekly and firm.

Do you offer high school credit?

LabLine is not an accredited high school. We provide a transcript and a course syllabus that many schools accept for credit, but you should confirm with your school in advance.

Can I switch courses mid-term?

Yes, within the first two weeks. After week two, switches require approval from the instructor of the receiving course.

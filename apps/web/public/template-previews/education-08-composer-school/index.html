Course of study

Six courses, three years.

A full three-year course of study taken at the student's pace. Most students complete two courses per term.

i.

Harmony & voice leading

The grammar of Western tonal music. Daily exercises, weekly chorale, and one short prelude at term's end.

ii.

How a term unfolds

Three weekly rituals.

i.

Private lesson, every Monday

A 60-minute one-on-one lesson with your composition teacher. Bring your sketches; leave with comments and a homework score.

ii.

Cohort seminar, every Friday

A two-hour live seminar with the cohort. Two students share a work-in-progress; the room responds with care.

iii.

Hearing the music

Every term ends with a Paris recording session. Your score is played by professional musicians and given to you as audio.

Questions

Common asks.

What level of music must I bring?

You should read music fluently and know your major and minor scales. Year I assumes no formal harmony background. Applicants submit a 30-second written exercise with their application.

Is the school accredited?

Composer School is not a university and does not grant degrees. Our diploma is recognized within the European new music community and several of our alumni hold positions in working ensembles.

What software do I need?

Any modern notation software is fine — Dorico, Sibelius, MuseScore, or Finale. You should also have a piano or MIDI keyboard available for work between lessons.

Are recordings included in the tuition?

End-of-term recording sessions are included for full-credit and diploma students. Audit students may purchase a session separately.

Is financial aid available?

Yes. A limited number of partial tuition grants are available each term. The application is included in the academic application form.

Companionship, care, and the long Tuesday afternoon.

Elder Circle pairs trained volunteer companions with isolated seniors, runs eight neighborhood day centers, and delivers in-home care to 4,200 elders across Maine. Our promise is simple: nobody we know spends the holidays alone.

What we do

Three programs, one circle.

One-to-one

Companion Pairings

Trained volunteers visit weekly for two to four hours. Coffee, walks, a card game, the grocery store. Twenty hours of training, background check, and matched by neighborhood.

1,840 pairs2.4hr avg / week
Day centers

Eight Neighborhood Houses

Open 8 AM to 4 PM, five days a week. Hot lunch, music room, gardening club, gentle exercise, and a quiet corner with the day's papers. Transportation is included.

8 locations1,140 daily seats
At home

In-Home Care Hours

Licensed caregivers provide bathing, meal prep, medication routines, and dignity-first personal care on a sliding-scale fee. Medicare and Medicaid accepted.

184k hours / yearSliding scale

Become a companion.

We need 200 new companions in 2026. Twenty hours of training, a background check, and a commitment to one regular visit a week. We match you by neighborhood and interest — a former teacher with a former teacher, a knitter with a knitter.

Companion training

Six evenings over three weeks. Listening skills, mobility help, mandatory-reporter training, end-of-life conversations.

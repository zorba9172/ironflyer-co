Why Cipher

Self-custody, without the booby traps.

Most "non-custodial" wallets ask you to sign blind. Cipher simulates every transaction, names every contract, and stops the dangerous ones before you confirm.

Signature simulation, every time

Before you sign, Cipher dry-runs the call against the current chain state and shows you what your wallet will gain, lose, or approve. No more blind signing.

Phishing defense baked in

Domain reputation, contract reputation, and known-scam blocklists run on-device. Cipher refuses to sign for blocklisted contracts unless you override with an explicit, typed warning.

Hardware-backed by default

Keys are stored in the Secure Enclave on iOS, StrongBox on Android, and via WebAuthn on desktop. Hardware wallets (Ledger, Trezor, Keystone) plug in with one click.

Features

Everything a daily wallet should have.

Multi-chain by default. Swap, bridge, stake, and connect to dapps without leaving the wallet. WalletConnect v2, EIP-1271, account abstraction — all supported.

⌗

Multi-chain accounts

One seed, 14 chains. Ethereum, Optimism, Arbitrum, Base, Polygon, zkSync, Scroll, Avalanche, BNB, Linea, and more.

↔

In-app swap

Aggregated routing across 0x, 1inch, and Cowswap. MEV-protected by default. Slippage and price impact shown before you sign.

⇄

Native bridge

Built-in cross-chain transfers via canonical bridges and Stargate. Native asset out, no wrapped IOUs.

⛬

Account abstraction

ERC-4337 smart accounts with social recovery, daily spend limits, and session keys. Opt in per account.

$

Stake from the wallet

One-tap staking for Lido, Rocket Pool, and EigenLayer. Rewards visible in your balance, never sent to a custodian.

⚙

Hardware wallet support

Ledger Stax, Ledger Nano, Trezor Safe, Keystone, and GridPlus over USB and Bluetooth. Sign on the device, never on the host.

Developers

Connect your dapp in under five minutes.

Standard WalletConnect v2 endpoint, EIP-1193 provider, and a typed JS SDK. Cipher honors EIP-6963 wallet discovery so users see your dapp pick the right wallet automatically.

connect.ts 
import { Cipher } from "@cipher/sdk";

const wallet = await Cipher.connect({
  appName: "Field & Format",
  chains: [1, 10, 8453], // ethereum, optimism, base
  features: { simulate: true, sessionKeys: true },
});

const sig = await wallet.signTypedData(message);
// Cipher shows the user: domain, types, message body, and risk verdict

// What we do

Three things, on repeat.

We pick an idea, build a real product in six months, and either spin it out or shelf it. We don’t consult, advise, or run accelerator cohorts. Studio in, company out.

// 01

Spec it

The studio writes the one-pager and the architecture doc. We interview twenty operators in the category. We kill the idea ourselves if it doesn’t hold — thirty percent of our specs never become a build.

// 02

Build it

Two engineers, one designer, one operator. Twenty-four weeks. A live product with the first ten customers paying real money. We don’t do MVPs — the spinout starts with revenue.

// 03

Ship it out

We recruit or appoint a founding CEO, transfer the cap table, sign the founder agreement, and the company is its own entity. The studio holds 30 to 40%. We stay on the board.

// Portfolio

Six companies currently in flight.

Each one was specced, built, and either spun out or wound down inside Pivot House. The studio retains 30–40% equity at spinout and stays on the board for the first three years.

Before a single feature is shipped, the team has to sign a paying design-partner contract. If they can’t, the build doesn’t start.

~5 weeks

// Capital

The studio fund.

Studio LPs are a mix of family offices, two strategic LPs from adjacent industries, and four operators-turned-LPs. We don’t take government money and we don’t syndicate from platforms.

/ Chart of the week

Twelve trillion off the European bank balance sheet.

Take the top twenty European lenders, weighted by Tier 1 capital, and the picture is unambiguous. Where the US banks have held their risk-weighted assets roughly flat, the European set have shed close to twelve trillion euros in three years.

Most of it is repo, derivatives, and trading book — areas the regulators are not actually trying to shrink. The interesting question is what gets dialled back up next, and by whom.

From the editor

"We try to do business writing the way the spreadsheet does it. Slowly, with a footnote."

Tradewinds is a reader-supported business publication. We do not run advertising, do not write up sponsored research, and do not accept paid placements in our newsletter. Every Friday essay is fact-checked twice, against the source documents we cite. When we are wrong, we publish the correction at the top of the next issue, at the same length as the original.

Our coverage skews European because that is where most of our newsroom sits, but the readership is global, and so are the deals we follow. The print quarterly comes out in February, May, August, and November.

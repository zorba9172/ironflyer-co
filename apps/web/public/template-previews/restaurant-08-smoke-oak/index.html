The pit

POST OAK. NO GAS.

The pit was welded by a man named Cleve in 2010, runs on post oak we split ourselves, and has not been off in fifteen years except for a hurricane in 2017. Earl Briggs has worked it since the third week we were open.

Brisket, ribs, pork shoulder — all dry-rubbed with salt and coarse black pepper. That's the rub. No injection. No glaze. No foil-and-pray. We cook it until it's done, we rest it until it's ready, we slice it when you order it.

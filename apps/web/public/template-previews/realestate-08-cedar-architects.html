Houses, drawn with the fewest possible lines.

Cedar is a residential architecture practice. We design single-family houses and the interiors that finish them. Twenty-two completed since 2014. We accept four new commissions a year.

— Cedar Architects, A.I.A.

Drawings, not renderings.

Every Cedar project begins as a line drawing on tracing paper. We do not photoreal-render houses before we have agreed on the geometry. The drawing is the contract.

Four phases. Twenty-two months, typical.

Cedar is a slow practice. We do not promise a six-month design schedule. The drawings ask for their own time, and we listen to them.

i.

Site & program

We walk the site in person, in two seasons, before drafting. Program is fixed only after.

2 — 4 months
ii.

Schematic design

Hand drawings, models in chipboard, and a tracing-paper iteration with the client every two weeks.

4 — 6 months
iii.

Design development

Construction documents, specifications, and material samples reviewed in person at the studio.

5 — 7 months
iv.

Construction administration

Weekly site visits by the principal-in-charge through completion. We are on the porch on day one.

10 — 14 months

A practice the size of the room it sits in.

Cedar is four architects working out of two studios — a converted clapboard cottage in Portland and a fifth-floor loft in Brooklyn. The principal walks every site, signs every drawing, and is in the room for every client meeting.

We do not subcontract design. We do not run a marketing department. The waiting list is open by referral, and currently runs about fourteen months from first call.

— Annette Brüggen, A.I.A., principal

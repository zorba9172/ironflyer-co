The house

Built by a forester, kept by his great-granddaughter.

Bartolomeo Cadonau, a forester from Bever, hauled the first stones up to this ledge in the summer of 1908. Four generations later we run nineteen rooms, three suites, a small spa, and a kitchen for sixty. We do not have a concierge — the family answers the door.

  • Wood-fired sauna cellar
  • Long-table breakfast 8–10 a.m.
  • Boot room with dryers by reception
  • Library & wood stove open all day
  • Ski & hike concierge by request
  • EV chargers 2 stations
  • Children welcome over 6
  • Dogs welcome €25 / night
Dining

A short menu, cooked from the valley.

Our cook, Roberta, writes a new card every Sunday based on what came up from the farms below. Breakfast is included. Dinner is open to guests and to walk-ins from the village if there is a seat.

The long table

One service, one menu, four courses. Sixty seats. €78 per guest, wine pairing €34.

The library bar

House cocktails, alpine herbs, sixty bottles of Bündner wine. Open 5 p.m. to midnight.

Breakfast

Bread from the village bakery, our cheese, mountain honey, and eggs from the Cadonau farm.

How to find the house.

By train into Pontresina, then a short funicular up to Muottas Muragl, then ten minutes on the marked path. We will pick you up if you call ahead. Helicopters land below the south meadow with 48 hours' notice.

Where the day actually starts.

The pancake breakfast at the playground from 9 a.m., a 5K fun run at 10, then the fair at 11. The mayor cuts the ribbon at 11 sharp. The mayor is also our librarian.

What's there

Eight reasons to walk over.

No tickets, no fences, no marketing pop-ups. Just the neighborhood, doing the things it likes doing in early summer.

♪

Two music stages

Folk and brass at noon, a local cover band at 4, a sundown headliner at 7.

★

Food row

Twelve food trucks and four neighborhood restaurants on Ridgeland Ave.

▸

Sixty makers

Local crafts, prints, ceramics, and the only honey stand we trust.

●

Kids zone

Free face paint, balloon animals, the dunk tank, and the fire truck.

▲

Library tent

Story time at 11, used-book sale all day, summer reading sign-ups.

◆

5K fun run

10 a.m. start at the gazebo. $15 to enter; free if you wear last year's shirt.

✺

Beer garden

21+ with ID. Two local breweries, two ciders, one neighborhood wine.

✦

Fireworks at dusk

8:30 p.m. over the lake. Best seats are the south lawn or the dock.

Donate

Keep it free.

The Oak Park Community Fair has been free for 23 years. Sponsors and small donations from neighbors pay for the stages, the portables, the library tent, and the fireworks.

Donations are tax-deductible. Receipt by email within ten minutes.

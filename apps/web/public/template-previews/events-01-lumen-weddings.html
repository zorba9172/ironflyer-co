The studio

The process

A planning year, paced.

A representative timeline for our Full Planning couples. The dates move; the sequence does not.

M–14

First conversation

An hour on a video call. We talk about how you want the day to feel, where you grew up, and what you absolutely do not want.

M–12

Venue + date locked

We tour two to three venues with you, lock the date, and put the deposit ledger in your hands.

M–9

Design direction

A printed direction book: palette, paper, florals, music, the food story, and a tabletop mockup on our studio table.

M–6

Vendors locked

Caterer, florist, music, photo, video, paper, beauty, transport. All signed, all on a shared calendar.

M–3

Guest logistics

Hotel blocks, welcome bags, ride loops, RSVP audits, dietary spreadsheet, seating draft one.

M–1

Final walkthrough

On-site walk with caterer + florist. Timeline locked to the minute. Vendor packets sent.

Day

We hold the day

You wake up, eat breakfast, and get married. We are at the venue at 7 a.m. and we leave when the last vendor is paid out.

Frequently asked

The honest answers.

How early should we reach out?

Full Planning couples usually book us 12 to 16 months ahead. We open inquiry windows twice a year, in February and August.

Do you travel?

Yes. We work primarily in Sonoma and Napa, and travel three weddings a year. Travel is billed at cost, no markup.

Do you take more than six couples?

No. Six is the number where we can be in the work without diluting attention. We will refer you to two studios we trust if our calendar is full.

What do you not do?

We do not officiate, we do not do flowers ourselves, and we do not run wedding-adjacent parties for couples we are not planning the wedding for.

Same-sex, interfaith, multi-day?

Yes, yes, and yes. Half of our 2025 calendar is multi-day. Two of our 2024 couples were interfaith. We are happy to share references.

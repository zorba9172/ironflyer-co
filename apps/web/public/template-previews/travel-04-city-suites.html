Lisbon · Chiado · 44 rooms

A small, dark hotel in a bright city.

Forty-four rooms cut into a nineteenth-century townhouse off Rua Garrett. Black marble lobby, a basement jazz bar, a rooftop with a view of the river, and a doorman who answers in four languages.

The hotel

A house, staffed like one.

Twenty-two staff for forty-four rooms. The doorman has worked on this corner for nineteen years. There is no concierge desk — the team is in the lobby, and they are reading the same newspaper as you.

Dining & bar

Two rooms we want you in.

The kitchen is run by chef Tiago Bonneton, who left a one-star kitchen in São Paulo to come home. The bar belongs to a small jazz trio four nights a week. Both rooms are open to non-residents.

Location

One street off Rua Garrett.

We are on a quiet street one block above Rua Garrett, three blocks from Cais do Sodré station. The yellow-tile facade is the only thing on the street, and the doorman is the easiest landmark in the city.

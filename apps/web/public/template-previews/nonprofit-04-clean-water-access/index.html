How a well happens

Four steps. One contract with the village.

We don't drop a pump and leave. Each well comes with a 10-year service agreement, a local water committee, and a monthly maintenance budget paid into a village-controlled account.

01 · ASSESS

Hydrogeology + community

Our geologists test viable sites. The village elects a 7-person water committee with paid stewards. No committee, no drill.

02 · DRILL

Drill and certify

Mobile rigs drill, case, and pressure-test. Water is lab-certified for fecal coliforms, arsenic, fluoride, and TDS before handover.

03 · HANDOVER

Train and endow

The water committee trains for 6 weeks. We endow a maintenance account funded for 10 years, controlled by the committee.

04 · MONITOR

10-year monitoring

Every well is GPS-tagged and reports flow and quality readings to our public map. Independent audits at years 3, 5, and 10.

Where we work

Eleven regions, local drilling teams everywhere.

All field staff are hired locally. Drill operators are paid above local market wages and graduate from our drilling academy in Lusaka.

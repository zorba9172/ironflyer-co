02 — About

How I work, in three short paragraphs.

I trained as an architect and switched to product design in 2018. The architecture training is still the most useful thing I bring to a project: I treat an interface like a building, with circulation, light, and a clear front door.

I work embedded with one team at a time, usually for two to three months. I sit in your standups. I write release notes. I'm not interested in shipping deliverables and leaving — I want the work to be defended internally when I'm gone.

My favourite projects are the ones where the team has a real product and not a clear surface yet. If you're shipping software that other people pay for, and you'd like it to feel more considered, we should talk.

03 — Practice

What I do, in plain language.

Three things, billed by the project, scoped before we start. I send a fixed-price proposal after a 45-minute call.

i.

Product surfaces

The screens your customers use. Onboarding, dashboards, settings, the parts that need to feel inevitable. Usually 6–10 weeks.

ii.

Marketing systems

The site that introduces the product. Pricing, story, and the small pages that do the heavy lifting. Usually 4–6 weeks.

iii.

Design partnership

A retainer for teams that need a senior eye on every release. Two days a week, three-month minimum.

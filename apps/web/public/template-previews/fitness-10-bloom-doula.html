Approach

Quietly informed. Never directive.

A doula isn't a medical provider. We're the person who knows your birth plan, your preferences, and the small comforts that make a long night easier — and who stays in the room.

Your choices, our preparation

We help you understand options and ask better questions. Decisions are always yours.

One doula, in person

You have a primary doula and one backup. The primary attends your birth in 95% of cases.

Affordable, honestly

Half of our spots each quarter are sliding scale. We never publicize who pays what.

What people ask first.

Do doulas replace partners or nurses?

No. A doula adds continuity. Your partner is the most important person in the room. The nurses change every shift. We're the one person who stays the whole time.

Does insurance cover this?

Some plans reimburse part of doula fees. We provide the documentation. HSA and FSA accounts accept doula services in most states, including Minnesota.

What if my labor starts before 37 weeks?

We're available, period. We start formal on-call at 37 weeks, but we don't disappear before that. Call anytime.

What does a backup doula mean?

If your primary doula is at another birth or out of town, the backup steps in. They've met you at one prenatal and know your plan. Backup attendance is rare (about 5% of our births).

Are you welcoming to LGBTQ+ families?

Yes — every doula on our team has supported queer and trans families. We use the language you use about yourself, your partner, and your body.

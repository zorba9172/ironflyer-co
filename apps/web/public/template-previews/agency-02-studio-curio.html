Identity systems
for objects worth keeping.

Studio Curio works with makers, heritage houses, and product companies on brands that age well. We’re typographers and editors at heart, and we’d rather get one wordmark right than ten landing pages wrong.

— Now booking through
the back half of 2026.

On the work

We’re partial to clients who outlast their category: a fifth-generation knife shop, a cookware company that mills its own steel, a publisher that still pays its writers per page.

The work we do is the work an in-house designer would do if they had the time, the budget, and one less stakeholder. We make wordmarks, monograms, type systems, packaging, and the occasional book.

Services — 04.01

Four ways the studio engages, chosen carefully.

01

Identity, complete

Naming, wordmark, typographic system, palette, and the rules that hold them together. Twelve to sixteen weeks. Documentation handed off in printable form.

02

Packaging & print

Bottle labels, boxes, hangtags, lookbooks, catalogs. We oversee press checks. We have opinions about paper.

03

Editorial design

Annual reports, anniversary books, periodicals, archive sites. Built for slow reading, not metrics dashboards.

04

Stewardship

An annual retainer for clients whose brands we already shaped. One review a quarter. One critique a month. No surprises.

Case study — 04.03 — N° 027

Verso Ceramics

A wordmark for a fifth-generation pottery, redrawn from a 1923 catalogue.

Verso Ceramics came to us with a 1950s logotype, a 1990s addendum, and a recent attempt that no one in the studio liked. We dug into the archive and found a 1923 trade catalogue whose typography deserved a second life.

The new identity is built on a custom wordmark drawn from that source, a single text face commissioned from a Berlin foundry, and a palette of three earth tones the kiln actually produces.

Client
Verso Ceramics — since 1898
Year
2026, ongoing
Engagement
Identity, packaging, site
Process — 04.04

How we move, roughly.

Every engagement is different, but most pass through four phases. We share writing weekly and visuals biweekly. No design lives in a hidden file.

i.

Listening

Four to six conversations with founders, makers, and the people who actually answer the customer service email. We read every piece of writing the company has ever published.

ii.

Writing

Before any visuals, a short positioning document. If we can’t write the brand in a paragraph, we don’t yet understand it well enough to draw it.

iii.

Drawing

Wordmarks, type pairings, palette. Two directions in printed form, in your office, on paper that matches the weight of the eventual packaging.

iv.

Handing over

A printed manual, source files, font licenses transferred in your name, and a half-day workshop with whoever will steward the work after we’ve gone.

How it works

A day at Mentor.

01

Morning standup

Twelve students, one mentor, one shared screen. You commit to what you're shipping by lunch, and we hold you to it.

02

Deep work + pair programming

Six hours of focused building with a partner. Every pull request gets reviewed before it merges to main.

03

End-of-day demo

Each student demos in front of the cohort. If it doesn't run on a fresh laptop, it didn't ship.

FAQ

Questions before you apply.

Do I need to know how to code already?

Yes, at a basic level. You should be comfortable building a small web page from scratch and writing a function that takes input and returns output. We run a short pre-course assessment after your interview.

How many hours per week is the program?

40–55 hours. It is full-time. About a third of our students hold part-time jobs in the evening, but we discourage anything more demanding during the cohort.

Is the cohort remote or in person?

Remote-first with two optional in-person weeks: kickoff in Austin and capstone demos in San Francisco. Travel is not required and not subsidized.

What happens if I fail a checkpoint?

You get one re-attempt per checkpoint. Two failed checkpoints means we pause your cohort and roll you into the next one at no extra charge.

Is there a refund policy?

Yes. Withdraw before the end of week two and you get a full refund. After that, refunds are pro-rated for the modules you have not started.

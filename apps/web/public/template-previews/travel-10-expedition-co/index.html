DAY-BY-DAY · SVALBARD SKI TRAVERSE

Fifteen days on the ice.

An indicative schedule from our April 2026 Svalbard departure. Conditions in the field can and will move days; this is the plan we leave the office with.

DAY 01

Arrive Longyearbyen

Kit check, group briefing at 4 p.m., dinner at Funktionærmessen.

78°13′ N
DAY 02

Helicopter to drop point

Pulks loaded, the team is on snow by lunchtime, first camp pitched at 16:00.

79°00′ N
DAY 03 – 05

Lomonosovfonna glacier

Three days hauling across the Lomonosov ice cap. Eight hours on skis a day.

79°12′ N
DAY 06

Newtontoppen base camp

Settle into the camp at 1,200 m, rest day, weather window assessment.

79°00′ N
DAY 07

Summit attempt · Newtontoppen

Alpine start. Summit at 1,717 m if conditions hold. Back to camp by 18:00.

79°00′ N
DAY 08 – 12

Return traverse

Five days back across the ice cap on a different line. Two cold-weather rest stops.

78°45′ N
DAY 13

Helicopter out

Pick up from the agreed coordinate. Hot showers and a beer in town by 22:00.

78°13′ N
DAY 14 – 15

Debrief & depart

Full team debrief, gear hand-back, group dinner. Onward flights from day 15.

78°13′ N
KIT & SUPPORT

Built in-house. Tested before you leave.

We own the gear we send into the field. Sleds, sleeping systems, satellite, and the doctor. Nothing is rented from a desk in Geneva, nothing is improvised in the field.

01 · GEAR

Our own sled & shelter

Pulks built in Tromsø, the four-man tunnel tent we have used for fourteen seasons.

02 · COMMS

Iridium + Garmin

Two satellite networks on every camp. Daily check-ins, family text relay from HQ.

03 · DOCTOR

A wilderness MD on every roster

Not a guide with a first-aid course. A registered MD, on the rope team with you.

04 · INSURANCE

Global Rescue underwritten

SAR included, $250k medical, no exclusions for above 6,000 m on Pamirs rosters.

The Winter Menu · Hyderabad

Nizam-era plates, cooked the slow way.

A short menu, six weeks at a time. Every dish is sourced and seasoned the way it would be in a home in Hyderabad's old city.

In the Kitchen

The chef and the road.

Priya Iyer was raised in Chennai, trained at Gaggan in Bangkok, ran the tasting menu at Indian Accent in New York. She opened Saffron in 2021 with one rule: the menu changes when she goes home.

Every season she travels — Punjab one winter, Kerala the next — and brings the kitchen with her. She cooks in homes, in market stalls, in temple kitchens. She writes the next menu in a notebook on the flight home.

This winter the menu is Hyderabad. Twelve dishes. The recipes came from a single home in Charminar. The aunties have been emailing notes.

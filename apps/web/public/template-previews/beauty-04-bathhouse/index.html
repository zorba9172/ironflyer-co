The rituals

A bathhouse is a sequence, not a place.

There's no wrong order. But there is a slow, traditional one. Move warm, then cold, then warm again, then rest. A whole afternoon is a perfectly reasonable amount of time to take.

탕 · the soak

Begin in the warm pool

Forty-degree mineral water. Twelve minutes, soft breath, no phone. Hand on the rim, head on the stone.

40°C water12 min first round
한증막 · the sweat

Sit in the kiln

A clay-walled hot room, 70°C. Lie flat. Let your shoulders unkink. Hydrate from the cold barley tea by the door.

70°C kiln10–15 min
냉탕 · the cold

Plunge once, then again

A small twelve-degree pool. In, hold, out. Your spine wakes up. Walk slowly to the next room.

12°C plunge30 sec & repeat
세신 · the scrub

The full-body seshin

A trained attendant, an Italy-towel, forty minutes of careful exfoliation. You leave a different person, in a softer skin.

40 minfrom $75
한식 · the rest

Sit, eat, sleep a little

Cold mul-naengmyeon noodles or hot sikhye. A floor mat in the rest room. People nap here. Genuinely.

Kitchen open11am — 10pm
차 · the close

End with tea

Roasted barley tea, hot or cold, included in your day pass. Take a paper cup home if you want.

Free, alwaysBy the lockers

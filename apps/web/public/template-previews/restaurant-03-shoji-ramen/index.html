The Broth

One broth. Thirty-six hours.

The tonkotsu starts at six in the morning on Sunday and is poured into bowls at lunch on Tuesday. Pork femurs and trotters from a single farm in Iowa, water, scallion tops, ginger, garlic. Nothing else.

It boils, hard, for a day and a half. It is skimmed every hour. By the second night the bones are soft, the broth is white, and the cook is tired. That is the whole secret.

How it works

Three signed steps. No surprises.

Vault Finance does not move funds. Vaults move funds, on-chain, within bounded strategies you can read in advance.

I

Deposit into a vault

Pick a vault. Read its strategy report and audit. Deposit your asset. You receive vault shares (ERC-4626) that track your portion of the position.

II

Strategy compounds

The curator's strategy executes on-chain within the vault's defined bounds. Every action emits an event. Every fee is published. There is no off-chain accounting.

III

Withdraw, on demand

Burn your shares and receive the underlying asset plus accrued yield. Withdrawals are instant on liquid vaults; deferred vaults publish a redemption queue.

Strategy

Built for serious capital.

If you've ever lost money to a "rebasing" token, an opaque LP, or a vault that wrapped your funds in something you didn't sign up for — this is the antidote.

ERC-4626 shares

Every vault is a standard tokenized vault. Your position is a transferable, queryable ERC-20.

Bounded strategies

Each vault publishes its allowed contracts, asset list, and leverage cap. Off-bound actions revert on-chain.

Curator skin in the game

Curators co-deposit and earn a performance fee. Bad performance moves their share down with yours.

No emergency mint

The protocol cannot mint shares to bail itself out. The supply curve is a function of deposits only.

Reentrancy-locked

OpenZeppelin's ReentrancyGuard on every external call. State invariants are checked on every transition.

Transparent fees

10% performance, 0% management on Tier A. Higher tiers carry strategy fees, all listed in the vault contract.

Security

Audited. Insured. Bounty-backed.

Every vault carries at least one major-firm audit and a $1M bug bounty. Optional Nexus Mutual cover is available through the deposit flow on Tier A vaults.

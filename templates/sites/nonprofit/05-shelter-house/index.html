Shelter House runs three permanent supportive-housing buildings and a 24-hour street outreach team across Greater Cleveland. In 2025 we moved 1,840 of our neighbors from the street to a key of their own.

Our model

We give the key first. Then we work on everything else.

Housing First is not a slogan, it's a sequence. Mental-health care, addiction recovery, and job training all work better once a person has a door that locks. The data says so. So does every neighbor we've served.

PILLAR 01

Permanent supportive housing

Three buildings, 412 units. No sobriety test, no income test, no time limit. Wraparound services on site: nurse, case manager, peer specialist.

PILLAR 02

24-hour outreach

Two vans, twelve outreach workers, every night of the year. We know our neighbors by name and they know us. No quotas, no sweeps.

PILLAR 03

Path back to work

An on-site work program with three local employers. 68% of residents who join the program are employed and housed at 18 months.

What we do

Six programs, one front door.

Housing

Maple Avenue Apartments

148 permanent supportive units. Renovated 2022. On-site nurse, case manager, peer specialist. No sobriety requirement.

148 units · Opened 2017 · 96% occupancy
Outreach

Night Outreach Vans

Two vans on the street every night, year-round. Coffee, blankets, naloxone, a friendly face, and a direct line to a bed if someone wants one.

12 staff · 1,200 contacts / month
Day Services

Riverside Day Center

Showers, laundry, mail, medical clinic, and three hot meals a day. Open 6 AM to 10 PM, 365 days a year. No ID required.

214k meals served in 2025
Workforce

Path Back to Work

Twelve-week paid apprenticeship in building maintenance, food service, or peer counseling. Direct hire pipeline to three Cleveland employers.

68% housed + employed at 18mo

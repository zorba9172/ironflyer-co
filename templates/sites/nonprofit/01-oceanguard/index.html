What we do

Five programs, one ocean.

Our work is organized around the chain that breaks the ocean and the chain that repairs it. We fund both the recovery vessels and the policy work that prevents the next spill.

Plastic Recovery Fleet

Six vessels operating in the North Pacific, South Atlantic, and Andaman gyres. Each ship reports recovered tonnage weekly and routes recovered plastic to verified recyclers.

$1.20 / kg recovered6 vessels

Reef Restoration Corps

Trained local divers grow and outplant heat-resistant coral fragments. 218 reef sections restored in 2025 — average survival rate at 18 months is 71%.

71% survival @ 18mo9 reef systems

Sanctuary Trust Fund

Long-term endowments that pay coastal communities to protect 3,420 hectares of marine sanctuary. Communities receive direct revenue from sanctuary stewardship.

3,420 ha protected22 communities

Coastal Water Labs

Twelve open-data labs running monthly water-quality panels along high-risk coastlines. All results are published within 14 days under an open license.

12 labsOpen data

Policy & Enforcement

Drafting and enforcing illegal-fishing legislation in partner nations. Independent observers on 84 commercial fleets verified compliance in 2025.

84 fleets observed11 bills

Coastal Schools Program

Marine literacy in 240 coastal schools across 6 countries. Students adopt a local cove, run beach surveys, and submit data to the Coastal Water Labs.

240 schools6 countries

§ 01 — Thesis

The discipline of print — tight grids, careful hierarchy, a pencil over every line — is the discipline most digital work is missing.

We were trained at print houses. We still set type the way we were taught: slowly, on a baseline, in a measured column. Then we put the same rigor into a product flow.

It isn’t nostalgia. It’s that the constraints of print — finite pages, fixed grids, a press date — produced a craft that survives translation to any medium. Our job is to bring that craft to whatever the client is shipping.

§ 02 — Disciplines

Eight things the studio does, none of them by accident.

i.

Identity

Logotype, type pairing, color, voice, and a printed manual. We design identities you could mail in the post.

ii.

Book design

Monographs, archives, catalogues raisonné, museum publications. We commission paper.

iii.

Periodicals

Magazines, quarterlies, journals. We run editorial templates that survive an editor change.

iv.

Marketing sites

One-pagers, multi-section, conversion-aware. We design them like a folded poster.

v.

Product interfaces

Dashboards, complex tables, settings pages. The places nobody else wants to work on.

vi.

Type commissions

We commission custom typefaces from foundries we trust, and we hold the license in your name.

vii.

Wayfinding

Building signage, exhibition graphics, retail. Quiet, durable systems for the physical world.

viii.

Stewardship

An annual retainer for clients we’ve already shaped. One review a quarter, no surprises.

§ 05 — Studio

Five permanent designers. One studio manager. No interns.

Field & Format was founded in 2011 by Maya Field and André Cardoso, who met at a print house in Porto. The studio has stayed small on purpose: we’d rather lose pitches than lose the calm.

We share a building with a letterpress in Lisbon and a small risograph studio in New Haven. Both are working presses, and yes, you can come visit.

— Maya Field & André Cardoso, partners

— A note from the studio

We’re slow on purpose. We work with two clients at a time, share a single inbox, and answer email twice a day — usually before the post arrives and after lunch.

That’s how we’ve been able to keep the work careful: every page sketched, every wordmark hand-set, every photograph chosen by us in your space. We don’t outsource, and we don’t hire. If we needed to hire, we’d turn the work down instead.

— How we work

Four phases, about three months.

i.

A visit

— week one

We come to you. A day at the shop, two more in the studio. We listen, take photographs, and write down what we’ve heard. By the end of the week, you have a short letter from us.

ii.

A sketch

— weeks two and three

One direction. Not three. A wordmark, a palette, a sample piece of print, all drawn quickly so we can talk before we’ve gotten precious. We share it printed, not in a deck.

iii.

The making

— weeks four through eleven

We finalize the identity, design the site, oversee print, and photograph the work in your space. You hear from us weekly, and you receive a printed proof at the midpoint.

iv.

The handing over

— week twelve

A printed manual, source files, a half day with whoever will be updating things, and a follow-up visit at six months — included — to see how the work is wearing.

— Who we are

Two people, one shed.

Hannah was a senior designer at a Brooklyn studio for nine years before she moved up the coast. Owen was a typographer at a small publisher. We met working on a cookbook in 2017, married in 2018, and started Salt & Pine in 2019.

We work out of a converted boat-builder’s shed on the water, with one dog, one letterpress, and a kettle that’s always going. We’d love to show you around if you’re ever passing through Boothbay.

— Hannah Pine & Owen Salt

— The premise / 01

The founder is the brand. The brand is the founder’s patience for one idea, repeated.

We’ve worked with sixty-something founder-led companies over fourteen years. The pattern is the same: the company tries to grow past the founder’s voice, the voice gets diluted, and someone is hired to “professionalize the brand.” A year later, the founder doesn’t recognize the company.

Hangar 12 sits next to the founder during that growth. We listen, write, edit, and occasionally push back. We don’t design logos. We work on the layer underneath, where the brand actually lives.

— That’s the practice. The rest of this page is detail.

Practice areas

Four ways the consultancy shows up.

Each engagement is built around the founder’s schedule. Most are quarterly, some are weekly, none are monthly — we’ve never made a monthly engagement work.

i.

Brand narrative

A written, founder-signed narrative document. Who you’re for. Why now. What changes when you win. We write it together, you sign it, and your hiring, fundraising, and marketing teams use it as the canonical source.

ii.

Voice & positioning

The tone that the company writes in — in product, in marketing, in customer email. We draft the voice guide, train your team, and edit live writing for ninety days afterward.

iii.

Founder ghostwriting

Op-eds, conference keynotes, internal memos, founder letters. We don’t put words in your mouth — we record you, listen, and write you back in your own voice with the noise edited out.

iv.

Advisory seat

A standing seat at your monthly review for two years. Critique, redirection, and the occasional “write that letter to the team yourself” reminder.

The consultancy

Three partners. Twelve engagements at a time. No more.

Hangar 12 was founded in 2012 by Tomás Cardona — an ex-strategy director at three category-defining brands — alongside Maya Penn and Daniel Reyes, both former senior writers at companies you’d know.

We deliberately stayed small. Every engagement is run by a partner, every word is written by the partner who owns the account, and no one on this site is a junior. We’ve turned down growth four times. We’ll probably turn it down again.

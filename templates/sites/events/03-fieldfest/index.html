Venue

The Crouse Farm.

90 acres of pasture, 28 miles southeast of Asheville.

The Crouse family has been farming the same ridge since 1924. We have been camping on it together since 2017. You can see Mount Pisgah from the Hilltop Stage on a clear evening.

Camping

Sleep on site.

Three campground tiers, all walking distance from the stages. Bring your own tent or rent a pre-pitched setup.

BYO Tent

$85 weekend

Bring your own tent and gear. Pitched in the Lower Field, ten minutes from the Hilltop Stage. Includes shared water spigots and shower trucks.

Car Camping

$135 weekend

Park beside your tent in the Vehicle Lot. Includes a 12'×20' tent footprint and water access. Quiet hours after 1 a.m.

Tent Hotel

$420 weekend

Show up to a pre-pitched canvas tent with two cots, two pillows, and an electrical hookup. Sleeps two. Limited to 80 tents.

FAQ

The honest answers.

Is the festival kid friendly?

Kids under 10 are free with a paying adult. There is a free kids' tent with shade, water, and supervised crafts from 11 a.m. to 6 p.m.

What if it rains?

We have rained in 2019, 2022, and 2023. The show went on each time. The Orchard Stage has a covered viewing area; the others are open-air. Refunds are not issued for weather.

Can I bring my own water?

Yes, sealed bottles up to 1L or empty refillable bottles. There are 14 water-fill stations.

Pets?

Only service animals. Please leave your dog with a sitter. We love your dog, the festival is not for them.

Accessibility?

Wheelchair routes between all three stages, viewing platforms at Hilltop and Pasture, ASL on the Hilltop Stage. Email access@fieldfest.fm to coordinate before the festival.

What about the food?

Twelve vendors, ten with vegetarian options, six with vegan. Allergens labeled at every booth.

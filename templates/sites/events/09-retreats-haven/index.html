Approach

What we believe a good offsite does.

An offsite is the most expensive week the company will run all year. It deserves more than a booked Airbnb and a Notion doc.

i.

Slow programming

Two long sessions a day, with real breaks between them. No 9-to-9 workshop blocks. The hallway is where the offsite actually happens.

ii.

Real food, real rooms

We do not book event hotels. We rent five private estates we have visited four times each, with kitchens we trust and rooms we have slept in.

iii.

Two facilitators on site

A program lead and a logistics lead are with you the whole week. The CEO does not run the offsite; the CEO attends it.

FAQ

Practical answers.

How early should we reach out?

Eight to twelve months for a five-day retreat at one of our five destinations. We have done four-month turns, but the destination is less likely to be one of our best.

Do you work outside your destinations?

Once or twice a year, for an existing client. We do not take new clients into destinations we have not visited four times.

Do you handle travel?

We coordinate with your existing travel program. We do not resell flights; we will tell you if your travel agency is the wrong fit.

What about partners and kids?

It depends on the team and the destination. We have planned both partners-included and team-only retreats. We will be honest about which works for your group.

Refund policy?

Half of the planning fee is non-refundable once we lock the venue. The rest is contingent on the timeline we publish in the contract. Force majeure clauses are clear and short.

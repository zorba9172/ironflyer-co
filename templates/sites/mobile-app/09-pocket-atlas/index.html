Why Pocket Atlas

An editorial travel app.

Most travel apps are review aggregators. Pocket Atlas is closer to a small magazine. Each city is written by one person who lives there, with maps you can use when the phone has no signal.

i.

Offline everything

The map, the guide, the photos, the notes. Download a city before the flight; it works in the metro, the desert, and the airplane.

ii.

One author per city

Lisbon by Inês, Marseille by Camille, Tbilisi by Mariam. Each guide has a voice, a hand, and an obvious set of preferences.

iii.

The notebook

A small leather notebook inside the app. Drop a pin, write a sentence, attach a photo. Yours, even when you uninstall.

iv.

Turn-by-turn walking

Walking directions, voice optional, that quietly route you through the prettier street instead of the shortest one.

v.

No reviews

Pocket Atlas doesn't show TripAdvisor ratings or star averages. The author's recommendation is the whole signal.

vi.

Multilingual menus

Snap a photo of a menu. Get a translation, and the dish's name in the local language so you can order it correctly.

FAQ

The back of the book.

How much do guides cost?

Four dollars a city, twenty-four dollars for the year. The annual pass includes every existing issue and the two new cities a year.

Who writes them?

One author per city — a journalist, a chef, a designer, a translator. Each one lives there. Each one's preferences are obvious and unapologetic.

Where do the maps come from?

OpenStreetMap, styled by us, cached to your phone before the flight. Twenty-six languages, including right-to-left scripts.

Can I export my notebook?

Yes. PDF or Markdown, photos included. Your notes are yours. No subscription required to take them with you.

Will you cover my city?

Probably. Suggest a city and an author. We pick the next slate every six months from the inbox.

Take a city with you.

The first guide is free. Pick a city, download offline, and pack the notebook.

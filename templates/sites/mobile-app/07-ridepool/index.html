Why RidePool

Carpool, finally, with friction removed.

RidePool is not a ride-hail. There's no driver economy, no surge pricing, and no strangers — just commuters trading the seat in their car for $4 and ten minutes of company.

⌂

Verified circles

Match with people whose work email or HOA membership we've verified. No anonymous strangers in the carpool.

⇄

Two-way matching

Set your home, your office, and your hours once. RidePool auto-matches both the morning and the evening, every weekday.

$

Fair split

The fee is the IRS mileage rate, split evenly. Drivers are reimbursed for fuel + wear, not turned into gig workers.

⏱

HOV lanes ready

Driving an HOV-eligible route? RidePool shows you the time-savings before you book. Often 15–25 minutes.

○

Cancel without drama

Each rider gets two no-fault cancels a month. Repeat flakers lose access. The whole system runs on small accountability.

Employer programs

FAQ

Reasonable questions.

Is RidePool free?

Free for verified commuters. RidePool takes a 6% platform fee from the per-trip reimbursement, and that's the only money in the loop.

Am I going to ride with strangers?

Not really. Match scope is "neighbors" (verified HOA / building / school) and "coworkers" (verified work email). Anonymous strangers are out of scope.

What if no one matches my hours?

Your search radius widens by 1 mile a day for up to a week. We'll also alert you when someone new joins your route.

Is this taxed as ride-share income?

Reimbursements at or below the IRS mileage rate are not income. RidePool keeps you below the threshold by default. If you cross it, we send you a 1099.

What about insurance?

Driver's existing personal auto policy covers carpool. We're a passenger-share, not a TNC. We do offer optional supplemental coverage for $4 a month.

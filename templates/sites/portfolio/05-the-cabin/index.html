The quiet machine in the next room.

Pre-order · ships October 2026

A Slow Ending

I started this book the autumn after my father died. It is partly about him, partly about Connemara, and almost entirely about why we still try to finish things in a world built to be unfinished. Two-hundred-and-twelve pages, no footnotes, one map.

The first chapter is available in full, free, here on the site. The book itself will be available wherever good books are sold, and signed copies are available directly from the press.

V. About

A short biography, written by me.

I'm Hana. I write essays from a small wooden cabin on the edge of Connemara, in the west of Ireland. Before this I was a science journalist in London for a decade, and before that I was a not-very-good philosophy student in Edinburgh. The Cabin began as a Sunday letter to twenty-two friends. It is now mostly the same letter, with more readers.

My books — The Slow Internet (2019), Inheritance (2022), and the forthcoming A Slow Ending — are all attempts at the same question, asked differently: what do we owe the things we made and then forgot to finish? The question covers software, marriages, and the village I now live in.

If you've found me through a friend, hello. If you've found me through an algorithm, I'm sorry. Either way, you're welcome here.

Our approach

We sell land we have walked.

A ranch is not an MLS listing. Water, easements, AUM, mineral rights, fence condition, and seasonal flow change the value far more than square footage. We walk every property in person, in season, before we represent it.

01

Tour the land in season

Spring runoff, summer pasture, fall sale weight. We see the property under load before we price it.

02

Build the property book

Survey, water rights, grazing leases, conservation easements, mineral history, comparable sales. Reviewed by counsel.

03

Match the buyer

Operating buyers, conservation buyers, and recreation buyers price differently. We start with the right one.

04

Close on the ground

We close in the county where the land sits. The owner shakes the buyer's hand on the porch, not in a title office two states away.

Owner stories

Five generations. Three decades of closings.

Selling a family ranch is rarely about price. It is about who the next steward is, and whether the new owner will keep the cattle running.

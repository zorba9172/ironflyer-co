From farm to your mailbox

How it works.

01.

Source

We buy direct or through importers we have worked with for years. Every lot is cupped twice in our roastery before it lands in the catalogue.

02.

Profile

Each lot gets a roast profile dialed in by our head roaster — usually somewhere between 7m20 and 9m40, depending on density.

03.

Roast Friday

We roast Fridays only, to a sales window we know. Your bag is dated with the morning it came out of the cooling tray.

04.

Ship Monday

Beans rest over the weekend, then go in the box first thing Monday. Most subscribers get coffee on a Tuesday.

The roastery

One drum. Three roasters.

We roast on a 1958 Probat we restored ourselves, in a converted dairy in the Hudson Valley. The capacity is small on purpose — we limit subscribers so we can roast for each order.

You will see our roasters' initials on the bag. That is who pulled the drop. If something is wrong, they will pick up the phone.

"Old coffee tastes old. A bag should arrive while the beans are still curious about being out of the roaster."

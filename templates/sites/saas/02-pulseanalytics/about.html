About PulseAnalytics

Analytics with a P&L in mind.

We were analytics engineers who watched the same charts get re-built every quarter from a slightly different CSV. Pulse is the answer we wish we'd had: warehouse-native, audit-grade, and aimed squarely at the people who sign the checks.

Origin

Why this company exists.

The trigger was a board meeting that went sideways. The CRO presented one revenue number. The CFO presented another. The product analytics tool, the BI stack, and the spreadsheet that fed the deck had all calculated MRR differently — and nobody could reproduce the rounding.

That night we sketched the contract: every chart should publish its method note, cite the warehouse table it read, name the assumptions, and produce the same number on Tuesday that it produced on Monday. Three months later we had a working warehouse-native analytics tool. Two years later we have an audit packet that survives a real audit.

Pulse exists because finance leaders deserve a chart with a paper trail. And because product teams deserve to know which feature paid for the quarter.

What we value

Six principles we won't compromise.

These show up in product reviews, hiring loops, and customer onboarding. If a feature breaks one of them, it doesn't ship.

01

Reproducible by default

Every chart is deterministic. The same query on the same data returns the same number, with the same method note attached.

02

Warehouse-native

We read your warehouse directly. We never copy your data. We never operate a parallel store you have to reconcile.

03

Honest comparisons

Our competitive table is updated the week competitors ship. If we lose on a dimension, we say so.

04

Auditors are users

The audit packet is a first-class export. Anyone — your auditor, your board, your CFO — can read it without a walkthrough.

05

Forecast with humility

Models ship with confidence intervals, not vibes. We will tell you when a scenario is outside training range.

06

Read-only is sacred

Pulse reads. Pulse never writes back to your warehouse. The bytes you send us are the bytes you'd send a SOC 2 report.

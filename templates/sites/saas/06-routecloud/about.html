// about routecloud

Built by SREs who got tired of half-true YAML.

RouteCloud was three site reliability engineers and a senior PM who had shipped infra for a decade and were tired of stitching console clicks into runbooks. We wanted a sentence to mean something.

// origin

WHY THIS company EXISTS.

The pattern that bothered us was a Slack message that said "ship the API to eu-west with a redis and a postgres." The next four hours were Terraform files, IAM policies, a half-broken pipeline, a $400 surprise on the bill, and a manual rollback at midnight.

RouteCloud is the answer to that Slack message. The sentence parses into a plan. The plan prices itself. The plan applies to real Terraform you can read and own. The URL comes back inside a hundred seconds. The rollback is another sentence.

We don't think CLIs are nostalgia. We think they're the shortest path between intent and a working URL. The keyboard remembers things the console forgets.

// values

SIX rules we SHIP against.

These aren't aspirations. They are the contracts we measure ourselves against — and the contracts our customers measure us by.

01 · plan

plans you can read

Every sentence renders to a structured plan. Resources, networks, IAM, regions. Reviewed before apply. Diffed on redeploy.

02 · price

cost before apply

The monthly bill is quoted from your cloud's actual pricing table before a single resource is provisioned. No surprises.

03 · own

your terraform, your keys

Resources land in your cloud account, your domain, your registry, your IAM. We are a planner, not a landlord.

04 · roll

rollback by sentence

Every deploy is a tagged state. Reverting is a sentence, not a war story. Postmortems get shorter.

05 · honest

honest compare

If a competitor catches up on a capability, we update the table. Fake checks poison trust.

06 · keys

break glass, leave the lock

Emergency access is a single command. It pages your security team, posts to audit, and rolls the credential when the window closes.

About InboxZero

Built by support leads who got tired of swamps.

We've all worked the queue. We've all stared at a wall of red. InboxZero is what we'd have built ourselves if we hadn't been busy answering tickets.

Origin

Why this company exists.

Our co-founder Avery ran customer experience at a fast-growing fintech. The team had three help-desk tools, four AI add-ons, and a Tuesday standup that was mostly people explaining why a thread was missed.

We tried every "AI inbox." They sorted by keyword and routed to whoever had the smallest queue. They didn't learn from outcomes. They didn't read your docs. They sent macros that hadn't landed well in a year.

InboxZero started as a weekend prototype that scored teammates by how their past replies on a topic actually performed — CSAT, reopen rate, follow-up chain length. The first time we ran it on a real mailbox, three threads moved to a teammate nobody had thought to assign them to. They all closed inside an hour. We've been building from that moment ever since.

What we value

Six commitments to the queue.

These show up in product reviews, ticket triage, and the way we treat our own support team.

★

Routing on outcomes

We score teammates by what closed, not what got picked up. Speed is a metric. Resolution is the metric.

✎

Drafts cite the doc

Every draft we write links to the page it came from. If the doc is wrong, we tell you. We don't paper over a stale macro.

⌗

Lead-first reports

The weekly digest is built for the support lead reading it on a Monday morning, not the dashboard architect reading it never.

⇆

Reversible by default

Routing decisions, drafts, and macros are all reversible from a single screen. Overrides train the model.

⊕

Privacy on the body

We summarize on read. We never store the raw email body. Your customers' words stay with your customers.

⏱

Honest comparisons

If a competitor catches up on a capability, we update the table. We won't sell a checkmark we don't ship.

/ A studio visit

Inside Atelier Halverson, on a Tuesday.

Three architects, one structural engineer, a kettle that gets used too many times a day, and a model wall that has not changed in seven years. A long visit with the practice behind this issue's cover project.

We sat in the studio for two full days. They built two models, ate one lunch, made fourteen phone calls, and at no point did anyone open a slide deck.

What we are, and what we are not.

Field Notes Quarterly is a print magazine of design and architecture, edited in Zurich and Mexico City and posted to forty-eight countries. Four issues a year, plus a single short letter on the last Friday of every month.

We publish projects that are built, finished, lived in, and at least one full year old. We pay every writer, photographer, and translator. We do not run advertising. The magazine is funded entirely by subscribers.

If a project has not been built, it does not go in the magazine. If we say something we later regret, we publish the correction in print, in the next issue, at the same size as the original.
